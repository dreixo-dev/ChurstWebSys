Statement of Faith

Our comprehensive declaration of the historic Christian faith

Our Theological Foundation

Emmanuel Christian Church stands firmly on the foundation of historic Christianity as revealed in the Holy Scriptures

We affirm the essential doctrines of Christianity that have been believed by Christians throughout history. Our statement of faith summarizes these core beliefs that guide our teaching, worship, and ministry.

The Ancient Creeds

We affirm these historic statements of Christian faith

The Apostles' Creed

I believe in God, the Father Almighty, Creator of heaven and earth.

I believe in Jesus Christ, His only Son, our Lord, who was conceived by the Holy Spirit, born of the Virgin Mary, suffered under Pontius Pilate, was crucified, died, and was buried; He descended to the dead. On the third day He rose again; He ascended into heaven, He is seated at the right hand of the Father, and He will come to judge the living and the dead.

I believe in the Holy Spirit, the holy catholic Church, the communion of saints, the forgiveness of sins, the resurrection of the body, and the life everlasting. Amen.

The Nicene Creed

We believe in one God, the Father Almighty, Maker of heaven and earth, and of all things visible and invisible.

And in one Lord Jesus Christ, the only-begotten Son of God, begotten of the Father before all worlds, God of God, Light of Light, very God of very God, begotten, not made, being of one substance with the Father; by whom all things were made.

And we believe in the Holy Spirit, the Lord and Giver of Life, who proceeds from the Father and the Son, who with the Father and the Son together is worshiped and glorified, who spoke by the prophets.

And we believe in one holy catholic and apostolic Church. We acknowledge one baptism for the remission of sins. And we look for the resurrection of the dead, and the life of the world to come. Amen.

Detailed Doctrinal Statements

Our comprehensive understanding of biblical theology

The Holy Scriptures

We believe that the Bible, consisting of the sixty-six books of the Old and New Testaments, is the inspired Word of God, inerrant in the original manuscripts, and the final authority in all matters of faith and practice.

"All Scripture is breathed out by God and profitable for teaching, for reproof, for correction, and for training in righteousness." - 2 Timothy 3:16

Humanity and Sin

We believe that humanity was created in the image of God but fell into sin through disobedience, resulting in spiritual death and separation from God. All human beings are born with a sinful nature.

"For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God." - Romans 3:23

Salvation

We believe that salvation is by God's grace alone, through faith alone, in Christ alone. It is based entirely on the finished work of Christ on the cross, not on human merit or works.

"For by grace you have been saved through faith. And this is not your own doing; it is the gift of God, not a result of works." - Ephesians 2:8-9

The Church

We believe that the Church is the body of Christ, composed of all true believers, and is called to worship God, build up believers, and proclaim the gospel to the world.

"And he put all things under his feet and gave him as head over all things to the church, which is his body." - Ephesians 1:22-23

Last Things

We believe in the personal, visible return of Jesus Christ, the resurrection of the dead, the final judgment, and the eternal blessedness of believers and eternal punishment of unbelievers.

"He will wipe away every tear from their eyes, and death shall be no more, neither shall there be mourning, nor crying, nor pain anymore." - Revelation 21:4

Reformed & Evangelical

Our theology is rooted in the Reformed tradition, emphasizing God's sovereignty in salvation and the authority of Scripture.

Sola Scriptura

Scripture alone is our final authority in all matters of faith and practice.

Sola Gratia

Salvation is by God's grace alone, not human merit or effort.

Sola Fide

We are justified through faith alone in Christ alone.

Solus Christus

Christ alone is our mediator and the only way to salvation.

Practical Implications

How our beliefs shape our church life and ministry

Expository Preaching

We prioritize preaching that explains and applies the biblical text in its context.

God-Centered Worship

Our worship focuses on glorifying God rather than entertaining people.

Biblical Community

We emphasize meaningful relationships and mutual care within the body of Christ.
