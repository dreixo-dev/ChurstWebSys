Core Doctrines

The essential beliefs that form the foundation of our Christian faith

The Holy Scriptures

We believe the Bible is the inspired, infallible, and authoritative Word of God. It is our final authority in all matters of faith and practice.

"All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness." - 2 Timothy 3:16

Salvation by Grace

We believe salvation is a free gift of God's grace, received through faith in Jesus Christ alone, not by works or human effort.

"For it is by grace you have been saved, through faithâ€”and this is not from yourselves, it is the gift of God." - Ephesians 2:8

The Church

We believe the Church is the body of Christ, composed of all true believers, called to worship God and fulfill the Great Commission.

"And he is the head of the body, the church; he is the beginning and the firstborn from among the dead." - Colossians 1:18

The Holy Spirit

We believe the Holy Spirit indwells every believer, empowering them for godly living and effective service in God's kingdom.

"And you also were included in Christ when you heard the message of truth, the gospel of your salvation. When you believed, you were marked in him with a seal, the promised Holy Spirit." - Ephesians 1:13

Christ's Return

We believe in the personal, visible return of Jesus Christ to establish His eternal kingdom and judge the living and the dead.

"He will come again in glory to judge the living and the dead, and his kingdom will have no end." - Apostles' Creed

Our Statement of Faith

Emmanuel Christian Church holds to the historic Christian faith as revealed in the Scriptures and summarized in the ancient creeds of the Church. We affirm the Apostles' Creed and the Nicene Creed as faithful expressions of biblical truth.

Our theology is Reformed and evangelical, emphasizing the sovereignty of God in salvation, the authority of Scripture, and the necessity of personal faith in Jesus Christ for salvation.

Sacraments & Ordinances

We practice two sacraments ordained by Christ: Baptism and the Lord's Supper (Communion).

Baptism: We baptize believers by immersion as a public declaration of faith in Christ and identification with His death, burial, and resurrection.

Communion: We regularly celebrate the Lord's Supper as a remembrance of Christ's sacrifice and a means of spiritual nourishment.
